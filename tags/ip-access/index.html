WordPress Security &#8211; protect your wp-admin with this quick .htaccess trick

Thursday, Apr 18, 2013 by Damien Saunders .htaccess, IP Access, Restriction, Security

WordPress is under going an attack by a swarm of Zombies– and they are after any site with an Admin user account. Securing WordPress is easy and you can do two things to protect your website and yourself. Change and then delete your Admin account (if you haven’t already) Only allow access to WordPress /wp-admin by your IP address Of course, you should also have a backup strategy — and so why not setup my plugin DBC Backup 2 to do the hard work.
